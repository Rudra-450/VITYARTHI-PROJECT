# Case Study: Identity Theft

## What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft is a cybercrime where someone illegally obtains and uses another person’s personal information—such as Aadhaar number, 
bank details, or login credentials—for financial gain or fraud.

## Scenario :
A college student in Bhopal, received an email claiming to be from his bank, asking him to “verify” his account due to suspicious activity. 
The email included a link to a fake website that looked identical to the bank’s official page. Trusting the message, Rohan entered his login ID, password, and OTP.

## How It Typically Happens (Step - by - Step ):

1. The attacker sends a phishing email or message.
2. The victim clicks on a malicious link.
3. A fake website collects sensitive information.
4. The attacker uses the stolen data to access bank accounts or create fake identities.
5. Money is withdrawn or fraudulent transactions are made.

## Who is targeted:
Students, elderly people, and individuals with limited digital awareness are common targets. 
People who frequently use online banking or shopping platforms are especially vulnerable.

## Consequences:
Rohan lost ₹45,000 from his bank account within hours. He also faced stress, had to file a police complaint, and spent weeks recovering his account. 
In severe cases, identity theft can damage credit scores, lead to legal issues, and cause long-term financial loss.

## Conclusion:
This case highlights the importance of digital literacy—never sharing personal information online, verifying sources, and using secure websites to prevent identity theft.
